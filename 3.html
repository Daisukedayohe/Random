Part 1: The Origin of the Universe

The Big Bang Theory

The leading theory: the universe began from a single, super-hot, dense point (a singularity) about 13.8 billion years ago and has been expanding ever since.

Steady State Theory

Suggests the universe has no beginning or end. As it expands, new matter is created to keep the density constant.

Oscillating Universe Theory

The universe undergoes endless cycles of a Big Bang (expansion) followed by a Big Crunch (collapse).

Mythological Origins

Before science, cultures created stories to explain creation (e.g., Egyptian, Kuba, Hindu, Visayan myths).

Part 2: The Origin of the Solar System

Encounter Hypothesis

A rogue star passed the Sun, pulling out material that formed planets. (Largely discarded).

Nebular Hypothesis

The solar system formed from a collapsing, spinning cloud of gas and dust (a nebula).

Protoplanet Hypothesis

Modern version! The nebula formed a disk where particles collided to form planetesimals, then protoplanets, and finally planets.

Part 3: Our Home, Planet Earth

How Earth Was Made

  • 1. Accretion: Formed from colliding planetesimals (~4.5 BYA).
  • 2. Differentiation: Heavy elements sank to form the core; lighter ones formed the mantle and crust.
  • 3. Moon Formation: A Mars-sized object crashed into Earth, creating the Moon from the debris.
  • 4. Water Arrives: Delivered by comets and asteroids.
  • 5. Atmosphere Develops: From volcanic gases and early life.

A Brief Timeline of Life

~3.8 BYA: First Life

Simple, single-celled organisms emerge.

~540 MYA: Cambrian Explosion

A huge burst of animal diversity.

~250-65 MYA: Age of Dinosaurs

Dinosaurs rule the Earth.

~65 MYA-Present: Age of Mammals

Mammals diversify after the dinosaurs' extinction.
