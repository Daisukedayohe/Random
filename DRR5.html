Hydrometeorological Hazards

Hydrometeorological hazards are caused by dangerous weather, atmospheric, or water-related processes.

Typhoon (Bagyo)

Intense circulating winds (>118 km/h). Lifecycle:

  1. Formative
    Disorganized clouds
  2. Immature
    Pressure drops, rotation develops
  3. Mature
    Max intensity, stable pressure
  4. Decaying
    Weakens, pressure rises

  • Signal 1 (30‚Äì60 kph): Slight damage, banana plants tilted.
  • Signal 2 (61‚Äì120 kph): Moderate damage, trees broken, rice/corn affected.
  • Signal 3 (121‚Äì170 kph): Heavy damage, old/light houses unroofed, big trees uprooted.
  • Signal 4 (171‚Äì220 kph): Very heavy damage, up to 75% structures destroyed.
  • Signal 5 (>220 kph): Catastrophic damage, most tall trees broken.

Other Hydromet Hazards

Thunderstorm: heavy rain, thunder & lightning.
Flood: excessive water on land (due to rain, poor drainage, low-lying).
Flash Flood: sudden strong flow on slopes/poor soil absorption.
Storm Surge: abnormal coastal water rise due to typhoon or seaquake.
Tornado (Ipo-ipo): rotating column of air touching land; 12‚Äì24 per year in PH (Mindanao, Central Luzon).

Monitoring Tools

  • Doppler Radar: measures rainfall, wind speed & direction; forecasts typhoons.
  • Automated Rain Gauge: detects excessive rainfall ‚Üí possible floods/flash floods.

Geological Hazards

Geological hazards are natural processes from the earth (involving soil, rock, or ground movement) that pose a danger to people, properties, and the environment.

Landslide

A Landslide (also called a Landslip) is ground movement on a sloping terrain, triggered by gravity and often exacerbated by heavy rainfall. Contributors and warning signs include:

  1. Water seepage, saturation
  2. Soil loosening near foundations
  3. New cracks/bulges
  4. Broken underground lines/vibrations
  5. Leaning poles, fences
  6. Silted creek flow

Sinkhole

Depression from collapse of surface over dissolved rock/weak ground. Worsened by flooding.

Signs

  • Landslides: new springs, cracks, leaning poles
  • Sinkholes: cloudy groundwater, sudden ponds, leaning posts

Tools

  • Abney level, laser range finder (landslides)
  • GPR (Ground Penetrating Radar): Detects underground cavities for sinkholes.

Preparedness & Response

Hydromet

Before

Stock food, water, meds, flashlight, radio. Secure roof & valuables. Plan evacuation.

During

Stay indoors, listen to advisories, unplug appliances, evacuate if told.

After

Return home only after clearance, report hazards, inspect house safely.

Geological

Before

Avoid steep slopes, plant trees, build diversion channels.

During

Move away, listen for cracking sounds, curl into a ball if trapped.

After

Avoid area, watch for flooding, report trapped people.
